## Part - 1: The Big Idea
### 1.1 - The One Sentence Test
A platform that helps students prototype their ideas by providing infrastructure that most Indian educational institutes fail to offer.  

### 1.2 - The Problem
\[ P-1 \] Who exactly faces it?  
- X: (Teenagers/engineering/college/school students)
Any person in X who wants to bring an idea into reality that involves a tangible entity face multiple challenges

\[ P-2 \] How often do they face it and how much does it (the 
problem, not the product) cost (in time/money/frustration) for them?
- Every time X gets a new idea, the idea is not translated into reality just because of the barrier to execution.
- A person X who's got an idea (that's related to an intangible entity) can execute it super fast because X needs very less resources or mostly no extra resources in most of the cases to execute it.
- Compared to the pace of execution of intagible ideas, tangible ideas are significantly slow, this creates a major bottleneck to human curiosity.
- Moreover to execute any tangible idea, one needs relatively larger chunck of money, higher patience levels to tolerate the delays caused by multiple points of failure and clarity on the execution which is quite often unclear and leads to frustration and giving up on the idea.

\[ P-3 \] What is the current solution to the problem? Why is it not 
good enough?
- A person X gets an idea (for ex., house cleaning robot which can help in maintenance), wants to implement this idea, so understands how to implement it based on the existing solutions and references available online.
- But for X to implement this idea, basic steps would be: 
    - procuring relevant components and materials (for ex., required electronics)
    - Testing them and figuring out how to get basic version of the idea working (for ex., making sure all the electronics are working)
    - Assembling and prototyping the idea - which will require tools and equipment which might not be available (for ex., soldering the electronics)
- While following the above steps might seem to work perfectly fine, but it's not that simple in reality, not everything goes right all the time, and if something goes wrong in the above steps - it causes loss of significant amount of money, time and energy - which prevents the execution of the idea 

### 1.3 - Solution
 Focus more on the end-user experience rather than technical details or business model. 
 - A person X with the above idea, instead of following multiple steps, leading to a multiple point of failure, rather just does one step
 - X goes to "MyLab" which simplifies the entire process and becomes a one stop solution thereby reducing the points of failure for the execution of the idea
 - MyLab procures the required components, provides the tesing facilities, prototyping tools, etc.
 - This helps in reducing the delay, lowering the points of failures, much more efficient execution, higher rate of success in implementing ideas and fosters the culture of building things in reality.

### 1.4 - The Customer
- A person X with an idea in his mind that has to be implemented in reality through the real-world physical entities. 
- Age: 18
- Situation: Needs to build a prototype for an engineering competition
- Habits: curious, tinkering around stuff, wants to build some product
- Frustrations: prototyping issues, procurement, tool unavailability, working area to build, etc.
